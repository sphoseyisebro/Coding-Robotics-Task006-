    <!-- Introduction -->
    <p>
        Ensuring health and safety in manufacturing is critical for protecting workers and maintaining a productive environment. 
        Manufacturers must identify potential hazards and implement measures to mitigate risks. 
        This involves regular training, compliance with safety regulations, and proactive management of workplace conditions.
    </p>

    <!-- Safety Hazards Heading -->
    <h2>Safety Hazards in the Manufacturing Industry</h2>
    <ul>
        <li>Exposure to hazardous substances</li>
        <li>Slips, trips, and falls</li>
        <li>Machinery-related injuries</li>
    </ul>

    <!-- 4 Essential Practices Heading -->
    <h2>4 Essential Practices for Enhancing Safety in the Manufacturing Sector</h2>
    <ol>
        <li>Provide ongoing safety training to ensure employees are up-to-date on best practices and emergency procedures.</li>
        <li>Regularly inspect equipment to detect wear and tear, and carry out maintenance to prevent accidents.</li>
        <li>Label hazardous materials clearly and store them in designated safe areas to minimize exposure risks.</li>
        <li>Ensure workers have access to and consistently use appropriate protective gear, such as helmets, gloves, and goggles.</li>
    </ol>
